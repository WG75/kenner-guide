Accessory Name: Darth Vader Cape

Category:
Soft Goods – Vinyl Cape

Used With:
- Darth Vader (Kenner)
- Regional variants (PBP, Lili Ledy, Glasslite, etc.)

--------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Darth Vader cape is a black vinyl soft goods accessory included with the Darth Vader figure.

It exists in multiple clearly defined mould variants (M1 onwards), with significant differences in:

- Shape
- Hole placement
- Surface texture
- Vinyl thickness
- Finish

This is one of the most complex soft goods accessories in the vintage Kenner line.

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTE

👉 Vader cape is NOT uniform  
👉 There are multiple mould families (M variants)  
👉 Structural differences matter more than colour  

--------------------------------------------------

MATERIAL

- Black vinyl
- Flexible but varies in thickness
- Can stiffen, crease, or warp over time

--------------------------------------------------

CORE IDENTIFICATION FEATURES

Key areas to inspect:

1. Cape shape (top curve and overall silhouette)
2. Neck hole spacing and size
3. Hole reinforcement (clean vs rough)
4. Surface texture (smooth vs grainy)
5. Vinyl thickness and stiffness
6. Edge cut quality

--------------------------------------------------

MOULD VARIANTS (M FAMILIES)

The Vader cape is broken into multiple mould families (commonly referred to as M1, M2, etc.).

Each has distinct physical traits.

-----------------------------------

M1

- Early production
- Cleaner cut edges
- Distinct hole placement
- Often thinner vinyl
- Smoother surface finish

-----------------------------------

M2

- Slight variation from M1
- Differences in:
  - Hole spacing
  - Top curve shape
- Minor material differences

-----------------------------------

M3

- Noticeable change in silhouette
- Slightly heavier vinyl feel
- Variations in edge trimming

-----------------------------------

M4+

- Progressive mould changes
- Increasing variation in:
  - Hole alignment
  - Surface texture
  - Thickness

-----------------------------------

LATER VARIANTS (MULTIPLE)

- Wider variation range
- Less consistency in finish
- Factory differences more visible
- Includes regional production variants

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR

- Black (standard)

Observed variations:

- Deep black
- Slightly greyed tone (wear or ageing)
- Surface dulling over time

IMPORTANT:
👉 Colour variation is usually wear-related, not mould-specific

--------------------------------------------------

HOLE DETAILS

- Two neck holes
- Variations include:
  - Spacing differences
  - Hole diameter differences
  - Clean vs rough punched edges

--------------------------------------------------

SHAPE VARIATION

Major identifier:

- Top curve profile differs across moulds
- Some are:
  - Narrower
  - Wider
  - More rounded
  - More angular

--------------------------------------------------

SURFACE TEXTURE

Varies by mould and factory:

- Smooth finish (earlier)
- Slight grain texture
- Rougher texture (later or regional)

--------------------------------------------------

EDGE FINISH

- Clean cut (early moulds)
- Slightly uneven or rough (later or regional)
- Flashing may be present on some examples

--------------------------------------------------

FACTORY ASSOCIATIONS

General tendencies (not absolute):

- Early Kenner → M1 / M2
- Unitoy / Kader → mid variants
- Lili Ledy / PBP / Glasslite → later or regional differences

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

1. Start with overall shape
2. Compare hole spacing and alignment
3. Check vinyl thickness and feel
4. Assess surface texture
5. Compare edge cut quality
6. Cross-reference with known M variants

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Assuming all Vader capes are the same
- Using colour as a primary identifier
- Ignoring mould family differences
- Not pairing cape correctly with figure COO

--------------------------------------------------

REPRODUCTION WARNING

Repro Vader capes often show:

- Incorrect shape
- Wrong hole placement
- Vinyl too thick or too thin
- Overly smooth or overly shiny surface
- Poor edge cutting

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

- Vader cape is one of the most varied soft goods items
- Correct pairing increases figure authenticity and value
- Advanced collectors track mould variants closely
