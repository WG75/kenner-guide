Accessory Name: Jawa Vinyl Cape

Category:
Soft Goods – Vinyl Cape

Used With:
- Jawa (vinyl cape version, early release)
- Primarily early Kenner Jawa figures

--------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Jawa vinyl cape is a brown vinyl soft goods accessory included with early Jawa figures before the switch to the cloth cloak version.

It is one of the most important early-run accessories in the vintage Star Wars line and exists in multiple mould variants.

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTE

👉 This is NOT the cloth Jawa cloak  
👉 This is the early VINYL CAPE version  
👉 Highly collectible due to short production run  

--------------------------------------------------

MATERIAL

- Thin brown vinyl
- Semi-flexible
- Can stiffen, warp, or crease with age

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR

Typical:

- Light brown
- Medium brown
- Orange-brown / reddish tone

IMPORTANT:
👉 Colour varies heavily due to ageing and production batches  
👉 Do NOT use colour alone to identify variants  

--------------------------------------------------

CORE IDENTIFICATION FEATURES

Focus on:

1. Arm hole size and shape
2. Arm hole spacing
3. Arm hole alignment
4. Top curve shape
5. Vinyl thickness
6. Surface texture
7. Edge cut quality

--------------------------------------------------

ARM HOLE CONFIGURATION (CRITICAL)

- Always two arm holes
- Key differences include:
  - Spacing between holes
  - Alignment (straight vs slightly offset)
  - Size and shape of holes
  - Clean vs rough punch edges

👉 This is one of the primary identifiers for mould variants

--------------------------------------------------

MOULD VARIANTS (M FAMILY)

The Jawa vinyl cape is commonly categorised into mould variants (M types).

-----------------------------------

M1

- Early production
- Cleaner hole punches
- Balanced spacing
- Smoother vinyl surface

-----------------------------------

M2

- Slight variation from M1
- Differences in:
  - Hole placement
  - Shape of top curve
- Subtle but visible side-by-side

-----------------------------------

ADDITIONAL VARIANTS

- Further mould differences exist
- Variations include:
  - Hole spacing inconsistencies
  - Slight changes in cape width
  - Different edge trimming quality

--------------------------------------------------

R VARIANTS (REFERENCE GROUPING)

Collectors often group additional cape variations using R classifications (R1–R11 etc.).

These represent:

- Production differences
- Batch variations
- Subtle mould or trimming changes

Examples include:

- R1, R2, R3… through to R11
- Differences often seen in:
  - Hole spacing
  - Vinyl tone
  - Edge trimming
  - Surface finish

👉 These are advanced collector classifications

--------------------------------------------------

SHAPE VARIATION

Key differences include:

- Top curve:
  - More rounded (early)
  - Flatter or wider (later)

- Overall silhouette:
  - Narrow vs wide
  - Short vs longer drop

--------------------------------------------------

SURFACE TEXTURE

Varies across examples:

- Smooth finish (early)
- Slight grain or texture (later or worn)
- Occasional imperfections from moulding

--------------------------------------------------

EDGE FINISH

- Clean cut (early)
- Slight roughness (later)
- Possible uneven trimming

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

1. Check arm hole spacing and alignment
2. Compare hole size and shape
3. Assess top curve shape
4. Evaluate vinyl thickness and feel
5. Look at edge cut quality
6. Cross-reference against M and R groupings

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Confusing vinyl cape with cloth cloak
- Using colour as primary identifier
- Ignoring hole spacing differences
- Assuming all vinyl capes are the same

--------------------------------------------------

REPRODUCTION WARNING

Repro Jawa vinyl capes often show:

- Incorrect hole spacing
- Wrong vinyl thickness
- Incorrect shape profile
- Bright or unnatural colour tone
- Poor edge finishing

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

- Vinyl cape Jawa is one of the most iconic early variants
- Short production run increases desirability
- Sub-variants (M and R types) are actively tracked by advanced collectors
