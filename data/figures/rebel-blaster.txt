Accessory Name: Rebel Blaster (DL-44)

Category:
Primary Weapon – Blaster Pistol 

Also known as: Rebel Blaster, Smugger Blaster. Smaugglers Blaster, Smugglers

Used With:
- Han Solo (primary)
- Luke Skywalker (later releases / certain variants)
- Other figures depending on region and production

--------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Rebel Blaster (DL-44) is one of the most recognisable weapons in the Kenner Star Wars line. It exists in multiple moulds and colour variations, produced across several factories including Kenner, Unitoy, Kader, Glasslite and others.

There are three main mould families:

- M1
- M2
- M3

Each mould has distinct physical traits that allow identification.

--------------------------------------------------

MOULD TYPES

M1 – EARLY MOULD

Key Traits:
- Sharper, more defined sculpt
- Clean lines and proportions
- More detailed barrel and scope

Colours:
- Dark grey
- Black
- Early blue-grey tones

Notes:
- Earliest production mould
- Often associated with early Han Solo releases
- Harder to find in high grade

--------------------------------------------------

M2 – STANDARD PRODUCTION MOULD

Key Traits:
- Slightly softer detail than M1
- Minor changes to barrel and grip shape
- More consistent manufacturing output

Colours:
- Dark blue
- Mid blue
- Grey-blue
- Black

Notes:
- Most common mould type
- Produced across multiple factories
- Wide variation in plastic tones due to factory differences

--------------------------------------------------

M3 – LATER / SIMPLIFIED MOULD

Key Traits:
- Noticeably softer sculpt
- Reduced detail in barrel and handle
- Slightly chunkier appearance

Colours:
- Blue
- Dark blue
- Black

Notes:
- Later production runs
- More variation in finish and sharpness

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR VARIATIONS

Common colour categories:

- Black
- Dark blue
- Mid blue
- Light blue / slate blue
- Grey / blue-grey
- Translucent blue (some factory runs)

Notes:
- Colour alone is NOT reliable for identification
- Lighting and ageing can distort perceived colour
- Always pair colour with mould traits

--------------------------------------------------

FACTORY VARIATION

Factories include:

- Kenner (Hong Kong / China)
- Unitoy
- Kader
- Glasslite (Brazil)
- PBP (Spain)
- Lili Ledy (Mexico)

Factory differences may affect:
- Plastic tone
- Surface finish
- Sharpness of detail

--------------------------------------------------

GLASSLITE VARIANTS

Glasslite Rebel Blasters can appear:

- Lighter in colour
- Slightly translucent
- With softer mould definition

Notes:
- Often confused with repros due to unusual appearance
- Must be judged in context with figure origin

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

To correctly identify a Rebel Blaster:

1. Identify mould family:
   - Check barrel thickness and scope detail
   - Compare grip shape and proportions

2. Assess colour:
   - Use as secondary confirmation only

3. Check plastic quality:
   - Gloss vs matte finish
   - Density and translucency

4. Match with figure:
   - Consider COO and factory origin

--------------------------------------------------

MATCHING TO FIGURES

- Han Solo → primary pairing across most releases
- Luke Skywalker → certain later variants
- Regional figures may include alternate pairings

Correct pairing depends on:
- COO (Country of Origin)
- Factory (Kader, Unitoy, etc.)
- Production era

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Identifying solely by colour
- Ignoring mould differences
- Assuming all blue blasters are the same
- Misidentifying Glasslite as reproduction
- Mixing mould types with incorrect figures

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

The DL-44 is a key accessory for value:

- Correct match significantly increases figure value
- Early moulds (M1) are more desirable
- Subtle differences separate average from high-end collecting

--------------------------------------------------

REPRODUCTION WARNING

Reproduction DL-44 blasters are common.

Watch for:
- Incorrect proportions
- Overly smooth or overly sharp detail
- Wrong plastic tone
- Lack of correct mould characteristics

Modern repros may appear:
- Bright coloured (orange, pink, green)
- Uniform in finish
- Too clean compared to vintage wear

--------------------------------------------------

NOTES

- Multiple moulds may have been produced simultaneously
- Factory tooling differences create legitimate variation
- Always cross-reference with figure COO when possible
