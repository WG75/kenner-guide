Figure Name: Jawa

Category:
Figure

First Release:
1978

---

Overview:

The Jawa is one of the earliest figures in the vintage Star Wars line and is best known for its distinctive body covering and early production variant using a vinyl cape.

It exists in multiple variants across different factories, COO markings, and production runs.

---

Accessories:

A Jawa is typically associated with two components:

1. Jawa Blaster
2. Body covering (vinyl cape or cloth cloak)

---

Body Covering (Key Identifier):

The most important variation is the body covering.

Vinyl Cape:
- Very early production version
- Brown vinyl (plastic)
- No stitching
- Short production run
- Rare and highly desirable

Cloth Cloak:
- Replaced the vinyl cape very early in production
- Fabric garment with hood and stitching
- Seen on the vast majority of figures
- Exists in multiple fabric and construction variants

Important Collector Note:
The switch from vinyl to cloth happened very early. Because of this, cloth cloaks are also found on early figures, while vinyl capes remain rare due to the short production window.

---

Jawa Blaster:

The standard weapon accessory is the Jawa blaster.

Known characteristics:
- Small compact pistol
- Produced in multiple mould variants (commonly referred to as M1, M2, M3)
- Colour can vary (black, dark blue, grey tones)
- Mould and colour differences may relate to factory and production period

Collector Note:
Blaster mould alone is not enough to identify a figure. It should be considered alongside COO, body covering, and overall figure characteristics.

---

COO (Country of Origin) Variants:

Jawa figures exist with multiple COO markings, including:

- Hong Kong
- Taiwan
- No COO (in some cases)

COO can influence:
- plastic tone
- sculpt details
- accessory pairing

---

Factory / Production Variation:

Different manufacturers produced Jawa figures and accessories.

This can result in:
- differences in cloak material and construction
- variations in blaster moulds
- subtle changes in sculpt and finish

---

Identification Approach:

Collectors typically identify a Jawa by:

1. Body covering (vinyl vs cloth)
2. COO marking
3. Cloak construction (fabric, stitching, weave)
4. Blaster mould and colour
5. Overall figure condition and sculpt details

---

Collector Importance:

The Jawa is one of the most recognisable and widely collected figures.

The vinyl cape Jawa is one of the standout early variants in the line and is heavily reproduced, making authentication important.

Cloth cloak Jawas represent the standard production version but still include a wide range of subtle and collectible variations.

---

Pairing Logic:

- Vinyl cape Jawas should only be paired with early production figures
- Cloth cloak Jawas represent the standard version across most of the line
- Correct pairing depends on COO, factory, and production period
