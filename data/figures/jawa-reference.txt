Figure Name: Jawa

Character Overview:
Jawas are a small scavenger species native to Tatooine. They operate as traders and scrap dealers, collecting and reselling droids and machinery. They are fully cloaked, with glowing yellow eyes visible beneath their hoods. Jawas first appeared in Star Wars (1977).

Collector Names / Aliases:
Jawa, Vinyl Cape Jawa, Cloth Cape Jawa, VC Jawa, CC Jawa, Small Jawa

Release Information:
- Released: 1978–1985
- Appears on: Star Wars, Empire Strikes Back, Return of the Jedi cardbacks
- Part of original 12 Back figures
- Early Bird mail-away figure (1978)

--------------------------------------------------

ACCESSORIES

Primary Accessories:
- Jawa Blaster
- Cape (Vinyl or Cloth depending on version)

Accessory Matching Rules:
- Cape type is the most important identification factor
- Blaster varies by mould and factory
- Vinyl vs cloth determines early vs later production

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTES

Jawa is one of the most iconic and misunderstood figures due to:

- Vinyl Cape vs Cloth Cape transition
- High reproduction rate (especially vinyl capes)
- Subtle sculpt and accessory differences

The Vinyl Cape Jawa is one of the most valuable figures in the entire vintage line.

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE TYPES (CORE IDENTIFICATION)

1. VINYL CAPE (EARLY)

- Thin, smooth PVC material
- Brown colour
- Drapes stiffly
- No stitching (cut material)

Key Traits:
- Glossy or semi-gloss finish
- Clean cut edges
- Often slightly warped with age

Important:
- One of the most reproduced accessories in the line
- Authenticity must be carefully verified

--------------------------------------------------

2. CLOTH CAPE (LATER)

- Woven fabric
- Rough texture
- Visible stitching or cut edges
- Softer drape

Key Traits:
- More natural movement
- Matte finish
- Frayed or soft edges

--------------------------------------------------

WHY THE CHANGE HAPPENED

- Vinyl cape likely removed due to cost and durability concerns
- Cloth cape cheaper and more durable
- Switch happened early in production (12 Back era)

--------------------------------------------------

VINYL CAPE VARIANTS

Factories:
- Kader
- Unitoy
- Taiwan
- Possibly others

Variation Factors:
- Thickness
- Cut shape
- Surface texture
- Hole spacing

Important:
- No single “correct” vinyl cape
- Must match figure traits

--------------------------------------------------

CLOTH CAPE VARIANTS

Mould/Pattern Variants:
- Multiple (M1–M7+ observed)

Differences include:
- Fabric weave
- Stitching method
- Shape
- Hood size

Material Behaviour:
- Shrinkage over time
- Colour variation due to ageing

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

To verify a Jawa cape:

Check:
1. Material (vinyl vs cloth)
2. Texture
3. Edge finishing
4. Shape and proportions
5. Age wear consistency

DO NOT rely on:
- Colour alone
- Thickness alone

--------------------------------------------------

JAWA BLASTER

General:
- Small compact blaster
- Distinct from Leia blaster
- Used only by Jawa

Colour Variants:
- Black-blue
- Black
- Grey-black
- Silver (Glasslite)

--------------------------------------------------

BLASTER MOULD VARIANTS

M1 – Unitoy  
- Long bump feature  
- Rough mould finish  

M2 – Kader / China / Glasslite  
- Short bump  
- Cleaner mould  

M3 – Kader  
- No bump  
- Smoother appearance  

Notes:
- Produced in high-volume sprue trees
- Wear affects surface detail heavily

--------------------------------------------------

FACTORY VARIATION (FIGURE)

COO Families:

Family I:
- Kader / Unitoy

Family II:
- Kader

Family III:
- Unitoy

Family IV:
- Taiwan

Family V:
- Poch / Lili Ledy variants

Key Traits:
- Subtle sculpt differences
- Colour variation in plastic
- Eye glow variation
- Hood sculpt differences

--------------------------------------------------

PRODUCTION LOGIC

Early:
- Vinyl cape
- Early blaster moulds

Later:
- Cloth cape
- More variation in blaster moulds

Regional:
- Lili Ledy → unique colour tones
- Glasslite → silver accessory variants

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Confusing reproduction vinyl capes for originals
- Assuming all vinyl capes are the same
- Ignoring cape when identifying figure
- Pairing incorrect blaster with figure
- Over-trusting COO without accessory match

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

Jawa is one of the most accessory-driven figures:

- The cape determines most of the value
- The figure itself is relatively simple
- Matching correct cape + blaster is critical

Vinyl Cape Jawa:
- High-value collector piece
- Requires careful authentication

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY (CRITICAL)

To correctly identify a Jawa:

You must combine:

1. Cape type (vinyl vs cloth)
2. Cape variant (shape, texture)
3. Blaster mould and colour
4. COO marking
5. Plastic colour and sculpt traits

Cape alone is not enough.
COO alone is not enough.
