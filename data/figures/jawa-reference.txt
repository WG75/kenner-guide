Figure Name: Jawa

Category:
Figure

First Release:
1978

---

Overview:

The Jawa is one of the earliest figures in the vintage Star Wars line and is best known for its distinctive body covering and early production vinyl cape variant.

It exists in multiple variants across different factories, COO markings, and production runs.

---

Accessories:

A Jawa is typically associated with two main accessories:

1. Jawa Blaster
2. Body covering:
   - Vinyl Cape
   - Cloth Cloak

---

Body Covering:

The body covering is the most obvious first visual difference.

Vinyl Cape:
- Very early production version
- Brown vinyl body covering
- Rare and highly desirable
- Short production run

Cloth Cloak:
- Standard production version
- Fabric garment with hood and stitching
- Exists in multiple construction variants

Important Collector Note:
The change from vinyl cape to cloth cloak happened very early, which is why vinyl cape Jawas are rare and valuable, while cloth cloak Jawas make up the vast majority of surviving examples.

---

Advanced Identification:

For serious collectors trying to match a Jawa correctly, the main starting point is not just vinyl vs cloth.

The key factors are:

1. COO (Country of Origin) on the figure
2. Cloak or cape type
3. Hood size and shape on cloth cloaks
4. Stitching and construction details
5. Blaster mould traits and markings

These details help determine:
- correct matching
- likely production origin
- whether parts are original or reproduction
- whether a figure is matched or mismatched

---

Jawa Blaster:

The standard weapon accessory is the Jawa blaster.

Known characteristics:
- Small compact pistol
- Multiple mould variants
- Colour variation across dark tones
- Markings and mould traits can help with identification

Collector Note:
Blaster identification should be used alongside the figure COO and body covering. It is not usually enough on its own.

---

COO (Country of Origin):

Jawa figures exist with multiple COO markings, including:
- Hong Kong
- Taiwan
- No COO (in some cases)

COO is one of the most important advanced identifiers because it helps narrow:
- production period
- factory family
- likely correct accessory pairing

---

Cloth Cloak Identification:

For cloth cloak Jawas, one of the most useful identifiers is the hood.

Collectors check:
- hood size
- hood shape
- stitching style
- fabric weave
- overall construction

These details can help match a cloak to the correct factory family and figure type.

---

Authentication:

Many collectors do not worry about perfect matching.

But for those who do, these details are important because they help separate:
- original accessories
- correct matches
- mismatched combinations
- reproductions

This takes practice, but it is one of the most useful areas of collector knowledge.

---

Collector Importance:

The Jawa is one of the most recognisable and widely collected figures in the line.

The vinyl cape Jawa is one of the standout early variants, while cloth cloak Jawas offer a wide range of subtle and collectible differences for advanced collectors.
