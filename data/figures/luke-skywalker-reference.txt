Figure Name: Luke Skywalker

Category:
Figure

First Release:
1978 (Early Bird Certificate Package)

---

Overview:

Luke Skywalker is one of the most important figures in the entire vintage Kenner Star Wars line.

He was one of the first four figures ever released and exists in a wide range of variants across multiple factories, countries of origin (COO), and production periods.

Because of this, Luke is one of the most studied and collected figures in the hobby.

---

Early Bird Release:

Luke Skywalker was included in the Early Bird Certificate Package.

- Shipped in early 1978 in a plain white mailer
- Not sold on a retail card initially
- Represents the first production run of the figure

---

Key Variant Feature (Critical):

Early Luke figures can come with a:

DOUBLE TELESCOPING LIGHTSABER (DT)

This is the most important identifying feature of the earliest Luke figures.

---

Double Telescoping (DT) Saber:

- Two-stage extending blade
- Inner filament extends from the outer blade
- Extremely fragile and often broken
- Quickly discontinued by Kenner

This makes DT Luke one of the most valuable and desirable figures in the line.

---

Transition to Single Telescoping (ST):

After DT sabers were removed, Luke figures were produced with:

Single Telescoping (ST) lightsabers

- One-piece extending blade
- Stronger and more durable
- Became the standard version

---

Accessory Pairing Rules:

VERY IMPORTANT:

- DT sabers ONLY belong with the earliest Luke figures
- ST sabers belong with later production Lukes

Incorrect pairing is one of the most common collector mistakes.

---

COO (Country of Origin) Variants:

Luke Skywalker exists with multiple COO markings:

- No COO (very early)
- Hong Kong
- Taiwan

Each COO can have:
- Different sculpt details
- Different plastic tones
- Different accessory pairings

---

Major Variant Differences:

Collectors track Luke variants based on:

1. Hair colour
   - Blonde (early)
   - Darker tones (later)

2. Face sculpt
   - Subtle differences in expression and mould

3. Torso and limb moulds
   - Small but important variations

4. COO markings

5. Lightsaber type (DT vs ST)

---

Why Luke Is Important:

- One of the first four figures ever made
- Contains one of the most famous variants (DT saber)
- Exists across the entire production timeline
- Essential figure for any serious collector

---

Condition Considerations:

Common issues include:

- Yellowing of plastic
- Paint wear (hair and face)
- Loose limbs
- Missing or incorrect lightsaber
- Broken saber tips

---

Collector Notes:

- DT Luke is one of the most faked figures in the hobby
- Many “DT sabers” are actually cut-down or reproduction pieces
- Correct matching of figure + saber is critical for value

---

Pairing Logic:

Early Luke:
- May have DT saber (rare)

Mid / Late Luke:
- Should have ST saber

DT sabers should NEVER be paired with later COO figures.
