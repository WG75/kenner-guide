Accessory Name: Imperial Blaster (E-11)

Category:
Primary Weapon – Imperial Figures

Used With:
- Stormtrooper
- Death Squad Commander / Star Destroyer Commander
- Other Imperial figures across the line

--------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Imperial Blaster (E-11) is one of the most widely produced accessories in the Kenner Star Wars line.

Despite being common, it exists in multiple mould families and sub-variants across different factories.

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTE

👉 There is NO single “correct” Imperial blaster  
👉 Correct identification = mould + colour + factory + context

--------------------------------------------------

MAIN MOULD FAMILIES

M1 – EARLY UNITOY

Key Traits:
- Long rear barrel “bump”
- Sharper sculpt and edges
- Slimmer proportions
- Visible gate scar near grip

Surface:
- Often slightly rough due to mould wear

Colours:
- Black
- Dark blue-black
- Greyish black

Collector Note:
- Early production
- More defined and desirable

Quick ID:
👉 Long bump + sharper detail = M1

--------------------------------------------------

M2 – KADER / CHINA / GLASSLITE

Key Traits:
- Shorter rear barrel “bump”
- Slightly thicker body
- Cleaner mould lines

Colours:
- Black
- Dark blue
- Blue-grey
- Grey
- Lighter tones (Glasslite)

Notes:
- Most widely distributed mould
- Produced across multiple factories

Quick ID:
👉 Short bump + balanced proportions = M2

--------------------------------------------------

M3 – LATER KADER

Key Traits:
- No rear bump
- Softer sculpt
- Chunkier appearance
- Reduced detail in scope and barrel

Colours:
- Dark blue
- Blue-grey
- Black

Notes:
- Later production
- Cost-reduced tooling

Quick ID:
👉 No bump + softer detail = M3

--------------------------------------------------

SUB-VARIANTS (IMPORTANT)

Within each mould, variations include:

- Scope shape differences
- Barrel thickness changes
- Grip proportions
- Muzzle detail variation
- Plastic flow and finish

👉 These are often factory-specific and not formally catalogued

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR VARIATION

Observed colours:

- Black
- Dark charcoal
- Blue-black
- Dark blue
- Mid blue
- Light blue-grey (Glasslite)
- Grey

IMPORTANT:
👉 Colour is secondary — always confirm mould first

--------------------------------------------------

FACTORY ASSOCIATION

UNITOY:
- Early M1 and some M2
- Darker tones

KADER:
- M2 and M3
- Wide colour range

GLASSLITE (Brazil):
- Lighter colours
- Blue-grey tones
- Sometimes semi-translucent appearance

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

1. Check rear bump:
   - Long = M1
   - Short = M2
   - None = M3

2. Check sculpt sharpness:
   - Sharp = early
   - Soft = later

3. Check proportions:
   - Slim = M1
   - Balanced = M2
   - Chunky = M3

4. Check colour:
   - Use as supporting evidence only

5. Match to figure:
   - COO and factory matter

--------------------------------------------------

MATCHING TO FIGURES

Casual Matching:
- Most Imperial figures accept multiple moulds

Advanced Matching:
- Early figures → more likely M1 / early M2
- Later figures → more likely M2 / M3
- Factory-specific figures → match mould accordingly

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Matching by colour only
- Ignoring mould differences
- Assuming all black blasters are identical
- Confusing Glasslite variants with repros

--------------------------------------------------

REPRODUCTION WARNING

Repro blasters often:

- Too smooth or too sharp
- Incorrect proportions
- Uniform plastic tone
- Lack correct mould characteristics

Bright colours (orange, pink, etc.) = modern repro

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

- Very common accessory, but highly variant-heavy
- Correct matching is subtle but important
- Advanced collectors focus on mould accuracy over colour
