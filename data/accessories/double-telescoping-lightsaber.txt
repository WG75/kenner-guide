Accessory Name: Double Telescoping Lightsaber (DT)

Category:
Accessory

Used With:
- Luke Skywalker (Early Bird and earliest production)
- Darth Vader (very early release)
- Obi-Wan Kenobi (very early release)

---

Overview:

The Double Telescoping Lightsaber (DT) is the earliest and rarest lightsaber type produced by Kenner.

It features a two-stage extending blade:
- First section extends from the arm
- Second inner filament extends from within the first blade

This design was quickly abandoned due to fragility and production issues.

---

Why It Matters:

- One of the most important accessories in vintage Star Wars collecting
- Found only on the earliest figures
- Extremely rare in complete form
- Frequently broken, shortened, or missing the inner filament

---

Core Structure:

A DT lightsaber consists of:
- Hilt (solid plastic handle)
- Outer blade
- Inner telescoping filament

Most surviving examples are:
- Missing inner filament
- Snapped at the tip
- Trimmed or repaired

---

Known Variants:

There are two primary DT types:

---

M1 – “Circled DT” (Unitoy associated)

Key features:
- Slightly thicker outer blade
- Surface may show a subtle “circle” texture effect
- No letter markings on hilt
- Often associated with early Unitoy production

Notes:
- Often considered the earliest DT type
- Found on early Luke Skywalker figures
- Typically yellow in colour

---

M2 – “Lettered DT”

Key features:
- Letter markings near the hilt (can appear on left or right side)
- Slightly different mould profile vs M1
- Produced using multi-cavity tooling
- Inner filament often shorter than M1 examples

Notes:
- Found across early production runs
- Can be associated with multiple factories
- Colour ranges from pale yellow to deeper yellow

---

Important Distinction:

M1 vs M2 is NOT just colour — it is mould structure:
- Shape
- Surface texture
- Markings

---

Condition & Authenticity:

Collectors must assess:

1. Inner filament presence
2. Blade length consistency
3. Plastic colour and ageing
4. Mould details (M1 vs M2)
5. Signs of trimming or reproduction

---

Common Issues:

- Inner filament snapped off
- Blade cut down to look “complete”
- Reproduction sabers (very common in market)
- Discolouration from age

---

Collector Notes:

- Complete DT sabers are extremely rare
- Matching correct DT type to correct figure increases value significantly
- DT sabers are one of the most faked accessories in the hobby

---

Pairing Logic:

DT sabers should ONLY be paired with:
- Very early Luke Skywalker
- Early Obi-Wan Kenobi
- Early Darth Vader

They are NOT correct for later carded releases.
