Accessory Name: Jawa Cloth Cloak

Category:
Clothing / Soft Goods

Used By:
Jawa

---

Description:

The cloth cloak is the standard body covering found on most Jawa figures. It replaced the earlier vinyl cape very early in production.

---

Key Characteristics:

- Fabric material
- Hooded garment
- Visible stitching
- Softer and more natural shape compared to vinyl

---

Collector Identification:

Cloth cloak variants can be identified by several factors:

- Fabric weave (fine vs coarse)
- Stitching style and placement
- Colour tone (light to dark brown)
- Hood size and shape (one of the most useful identifiers)

The hood is often a key distinguishing feature between variants, with differences in:
- width
- height
- overall shape and cut

---

Collector Notes:

Although often described as a “later” version, cloth cloaks appeared very early because the switch from vinyl to cloth happened quickly.

This is why:
- Most Jawas have cloth cloaks
- Cloth cloaks still exist in multiple early production variants

---

Variant Grouping:

Collectors group cloth cloaks into families based on construction traits, including:
- fabric type
- stitch pattern
- hood profile

These groupings help identify production batches and possible factory origin.

---

Collector Summary:

Cloth cloak Jawas represent the standard version of the figure, but include a wide range of subtle and collectible variations. Identification relies on close inspection of fabric, stitching, and especially hood shape.
