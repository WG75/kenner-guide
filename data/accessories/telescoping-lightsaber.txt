Accessory Name: Telescoping Lightsaber (Single Telescoping / ST)

Category:
Accessory

Used With:
- Luke Skywalker
- Darth Vader
- Obi-Wan Kenobi

---

Overview:

The Telescoping Lightsaber (ST) replaced the earlier Double Telescoping (DT) design.

It features a single extending blade that slides out from the arm, without the fragile inner filament found in DT versions.

This became the standard lightsaber type used across the majority of the vintage Kenner line.

---

Why It Exists:

The original DT lightsaber design proved too fragile in real-world use.

Kenner simplified the design:
- Removed the inner filament
- Strengthened the blade
- Improved durability for mass production

---

Core Structure:

An ST lightsaber consists of:
- Hilt (fixed plastic section)
- Single extending blade

No secondary inner filament is present.

---

Production Evolution:

Lightsabers evolved in this order:

1. Double Telescoping (DT)
   - Two-stage blade
   - Very early releases only
   - Extremely rare

2. Early Single Telescoping (ST)
   - Transitional stage after DT removal
   - Slight mould variation overlap

3. Standard Telescoping (ST)
   - Main production version
   - Used throughout the line lifecycle

---

Colour Variants:

Lightsabers were produced in multiple colours depending on figure:

Luke Skywalker:
- Yellow (most common)
- Pale yellow
- Darker yellow tones

Darth Vader:
- Red (various shades)

Obi-Wan Kenobi:
- Blue (less common than Luke/Vader variants)

---

Mould Variations:

Across production, multiple moulds were used:

- Differences in blade thickness
- Variations in tip shape
- Surface texture differences
- Hilt detail variation

Factories involved include:
- Unitoy
- Kader
- Smile
- Glasslite
- PBP / European production

---

Country of Origin (COO) Influence:

Different factories and COO markings affect which lightsaber is considered correct.

Matching the correct saber depends on:
- Figure COO
- Production period
- Factory origin

---

Condition & Wear:

Common issues:

- Blade stress whitening
- Warping
- Discolouration
- Tip rounding or flattening
- Snapped or shortened blades

---

Collector Notes:

- ST sabers are far more common than DT
- Correct pairing still matters for accuracy
- Subtle mould differences can affect value for advanced collectors

---

Key Distinction vs DT:

DT (Double Telescoping):
- Two-stage blade
- Inner filament
- Extremely rare

ST (Single Telescoping):
- One-piece extending blade
- Much more durable
- Standard production version

---

Pairing Logic:

ST sabers are correct for:
- Most Luke Skywalker figures (post-DT)
- Most Darth Vader figures
- Most Obi-Wan Kenobi figures

DT sabers should NOT be paired with standard production figures.
