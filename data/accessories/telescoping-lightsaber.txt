Accessory Name: Single Telescoping Lightsaber

Category:
Primary Weapon – Early Kenner Line

Used With:
- Luke Skywalker
- Darth Vader
- Ben (Obi-Wan) Kenobi

--------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Single Telescoping Lightsaber replaced the earlier Double Telescoping version early in production. It became the standard lightsaber type used throughout the vintage Kenner Star Wars line.

Unlike DT sabers, the blade consists of a single extendable piece.

--------------------------------------------------

Yellow:
- Luke Skywalker

- Darth Vader

Blue:

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTE

👉 Colour alone is NOT enough to identify a correct saber  
👉 Mould type + factory origin is critical

--------------------------------------------------

MAIN MOULD VARIANTS

There are 10 primary mould types (M1–M10), plus subvariants.

--------------------------------------------------

M1 – Flat Transition, Lettered Hilt

- Earliest mass-production single telescoping saber
- Used across ALL major factories:
  - Kader
  - Unitoy
  - Smile
  - Taiwan

Traits:
- Flat transition between blade and hilt
- Letter stamped on hilt (cavity marking)
- Often found on early 12-back figures

Water Test:
- Sinks

--------------------------------------------------

M2 – Flat Transition, Unlettered Hilt (Unitoy)

- Produced by Unitoy (Hong Kong)

Traits:
- No letters on hilt
- Slightly thicker build than M1
- Found mainly from 12C–21 backs

Water Test:
- Floats

Colours:
- Blue
- Red
- Yellow (translucent variants exist)

--------------------------------------------------

M3 – Flat Transition, Thin Tip (Unitoy)

- Later Unitoy production

Traits:
- Thinner blade tip
- Multiple subtle subvariants
- Often shows injection issues (dents)

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

M4 – Takara (Japan Exclusive)

Traits:
- Shorter shaft
- Unique sculpt
- Only appears with Takara figures

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

M5 – Round Transition, Wide Shaft (Kader / Smile / PBP)

Traits:
- Rounded transition (not flat)
- Thicker shaft
- Used later in production

Subvariant:
- M5b (longer version, sometimes floats)

Water Test:
- Usually sinks (except M5b)

--------------------------------------------------

M6 – Unknown Factory (Likely Palitoy)

Traits:
- Seen on Trilogo releases
- Lower detail quality
- No fluorescence under UV

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

M7 – Taiwan

Traits:
- Distinct EPM (ejection pin marks)
- Appears early (even 12-back era)
- Small vs large EPM variants

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

M8 – Glasslite (Brazil)

Traits:
- Unique mould
- Wide gate scar on hilt
- Exclusive to Glasslite figures

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

M9 – Lili Ledy (Mexico)

Traits:
- Lettered hilts
- Slightly different sculpt proportions
- Used on later Mexican releases

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

M10 – Top Toys (Argentina)

Traits:
- EPM located on shaft
- Rare regional variant

Water Test:
- Floats

--------------------------------------------------

PRODUCTION LOGIC

Originally:
- Moulds likely shared across factories

Over time:
- Each factory developed its own mould variations

Result:
👉 No single “correct saber” without knowing:
- Figure
- COO
- Production stage

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

To correctly identify a lightsaber:

Check:

1. Transition type:
   - Flat vs Round vs Smooth

2. Hilt markings:
   - Lettered vs Unlettered

3. Blade thickness:
   - Thick vs Thin tip

4. EPM (ejection pin marks)

5. Plastic feel:
   - Rigid vs flexible

6. Water test:
   - Sink vs Float

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Matching by colour only
- Ignoring mould differences
- Assuming all yellow sabers are Luke correct
- Confusing repro sabers with M5 or M3

--------------------------------------------------

REPRODUCTION WARNING

Reproduction sabers often:

- Look closest to M5 variants
- Lack fine mould detail
- Have incorrect tip proportions
- May feel too rigid or too soft
