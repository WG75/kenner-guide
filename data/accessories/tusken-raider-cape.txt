Accessory Name: Tusken Raider Cape

Category:
Soft Goods – Vinyl Cape

Used With:
- Tusken Raider (Sand People) figure
- Kenner and international variants

--------------------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

The Tusken Raider cape is a light beige vinyl soft goods accessory included with the Tusken Raider figure.

It exists in multiple mould variants (M families), with differences in:

- Arm hole configuration
- Shape and proportions
- Edge finishing
- Vinyl thickness

Compared to other vinyl capes, Tusken capes are:

- Lighter in colour
- Slightly stiffer in feel (in some variants)
- Easier to differentiate visually due to hole layout

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTE

👉 All Tusken capes use arm holes (not neck holes)  
👉 Hole spacing and alignment are key identifiers  
👉 Colour is NOT a reliable identifier on its own  

--------------------------------------------------

MATERIAL

- Light beige / off-white vinyl
- Semi-flexible
- Can stiffen, curl, or warp over time

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR

Typical:

- Beige
- Cream
- Light tan

Observed variations:

- Slight yellowing
- Slight darkening with age

IMPORTANT:
👉 Colour variation is mostly ageing-related  

--------------------------------------------------

CORE IDENTIFICATION FEATURES

Focus on:

1. Arm hole spacing
2. Arm hole alignment
3. Arm hole size and shape
4. Overall shape and width
5. Top curve profile
6. Edge cut quality
7. Vinyl thickness

--------------------------------------------------

ARM HOLE CONFIGURATION (CRITICAL)

- Always two arm holes
- Variations include:
  - Narrow vs wide spacing
  - Straight vs slightly offset alignment
  - Differences in hole size
  - Clean vs rough punch edges

👉 Primary identifier for Tusken cape variants

--------------------------------------------------

MOULD VARIANTS (M FAMILIES)

Tusken capes are grouped into multiple mould variants.

-----------------------------------

M1

- Early production
- Balanced hole spacing
- Clean cut edges
- Symmetrical shape

-----------------------------------

M2

- Variation from M1
- Slight changes in:
  - Hole spacing
  - Curve profile

-----------------------------------

M3

- Noticeable change in:
  - Width and proportions
  - Hole positioning

-----------------------------------

M4+

- Additional mould variants
- Increasing variation in:
  - Shape
  - Hole alignment
  - Edge finish

-----------------------------------

LATER / REGIONAL VARIANTS

- Less consistent finishing
- Slight variations in:
  - Vinyl tone
  - Hole punch quality
  - Edge trimming

--------------------------------------------------

SHAPE VARIATION

Key differences:

- Top curve:
  - Rounded (early)
  - Slightly flatter or wider (later)

- Overall silhouette:
  - Narrow vs wider capes
  - Shorter vs longer drop

--------------------------------------------------

EDGE FINISH

- Clean cut (early)
- Slight roughness (later)
- Occasional uneven trimming

--------------------------------------------------

SURFACE

- Generally smooth
- Some examples show:
  - Minor texture
  - Mould imperfections

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

1. Compare arm hole spacing
2. Check alignment (straight vs offset)
3. Assess overall width and shape
4. Examine edge cut quality
5. Evaluate vinyl thickness
6. Cross-reference with M variants

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Using colour as primary identifier
- Ignoring hole spacing differences
- Assuming all Tusken capes are identical
- Confusing ageing with variant differences

--------------------------------------------------

REPRODUCTION WARNING

Repro Tusken capes often show:

- Incorrect hole spacing
- Wrong proportions
- Vinyl too thick or too thin
- Bright or unnatural colour tone
- Poor edge finishing

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

- Tusken cape variants are easier to distinguish than Leia capes
- Hole spacing is the fastest way to identify variants
- Correct pairing improves figure accuracy and value
