Topic: Country of Origin (COO)

Category:
General Reference / Figure Identification

Overview:
COO stands for "Country of Origin" and is typically stamped onto a vintage Star Wars figure. It indicates where the figure was manufactured, not necessarily where it was sold.

What COO Includes:
Most COO markings include:
- Copyright year (e.g. 1977, 1980)
- Country (e.g. Hong Kong, Taiwan, China, Macau)
- Sometimes manufacturer markings

Key COO Regions:

Hong Kong (HK):
One of the earliest and most common COO markings. Early figures are often associated with Hong Kong production.

Taiwan:
Typically seen in mid-production figures. Often linked with specific mould changes and paint variations.

Macau:
Less common. Appears on certain figures and variants.

China:
Later production COO. Often associated with Return of the Jedi era figures.

No COO:
Some figures have no COO marking. This can be due to mould wear, removal during production changes, or intentional omission.

Important Collector Notes:

COO does NOT equal brand:
Figures were sold under different brand names depending on country (Kenner, Palitoy, Meccano, PBP, etc.), but the COO refers only to where the figure was made.

COO helps identify variants:
Different COO markings can indicate:
- different factories
- different moulds
- different production periods

COO alone is not enough:
Accurate identification requires combining COO with:
- sculpt details
- paint application
- accessory pairing
- mould characteristics

Collector Summary:
COO markings are one of the most important starting points for identifying figure variants, but must always be used alongside other physical details.
