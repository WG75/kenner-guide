Topic: Vendor Codes

Category:
General Reference / Manufacturing Identification

Overview:
Vendor codes are identifiers used to track which manufacturer or factory produced a figure or accessory. They are most commonly seen on packaging (cardbacks), but can also be inferred through mould characteristics and production traits.

What Vendor Codes Represent:
Vendor codes help identify:
- the factory or company responsible for production
- production shifts between manufacturers
- regional manufacturing differences

Common Manufacturers:

Kader:
One of the primary Hong Kong manufacturers. Produced a large portion of early figures and accessories.

Unitoy:
Another major Hong Kong manufacturer. Often associated with early production runs and specific mould styles.

Smile:
Later production manufacturer. Commonly associated with Return of the Jedi era items.

Universal Manufacturers (Taiwan):
Associated with Taiwan COO figures and specific mould families.

Other Manufacturers:
Additional companies such as Qualidux, Jetta, and others appear in later production or specific regional releases.

Important Collector Notes:

Vendor ≠ COO:
The vendor (manufacturer) and COO (country of origin) are related but not identical. Multiple vendors can operate within the same country.

Vendor affects variants:
Different manufacturers often used:
- different moulds
- different plastic mixes
- slightly different sculpt details

This results in identifiable variant differences.

Packaging vs Figure:
Vendor codes are most clearly documented on packaging, but collectors often infer vendor origin from:
- mould traits
- accessory characteristics
- known production patterns

Collector Summary:
Vendor identification is an advanced layer of collecting. When combined with COO, mould details, and accessories, it helps pinpoint specific production origins and variant types.
