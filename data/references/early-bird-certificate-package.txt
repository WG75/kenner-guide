Topic: Early Bird Certificate Package

Category:
Promotion / Packaging / Early Release History

Overview:
The Early Bird Certificate Package was Kenner’s Christmas 1977 solution to not having the Star Wars action figures ready in shops in time.

Star Wars took everyone by surprise, and because Kenner started late on production, they still wanted to get something into the hands of buyers for Christmas 1977. The result was the Early Bird Certificate Package, which promised the first Star Wars figures would be sent later.

How it worked:
Consumers bought the Early Bird Certificate Package in late 1977. It did not contain the figures themselves at the time of purchase. Instead, buyers later received the first four figures by mail in early 1978 in a plain white mailer.

The first four Early Bird figures were:
- Luke Skywalker
- Princess Leia Organa
- Chewbacca
- R2-D2

Important collector note:
These were the first four Star Wars action figures ever released by Kenner.

Variant significance:
The Luke Skywalker included in the Early Bird mailer is especially important. It featured the original arm-fed lightsaber with an additional double-telescoping tip that extended further than later Luke releases.

Why it matters:
Kenner quickly realised the extra telescoping tip was too fragile and easily broken, so it was removed from later production. That makes the Early Bird Luke one of the most notable and desirable early variants in the vintage Kenner line.

Packaging note:
The figures were mailed out in early 1978 in a plain white package rather than standard retail carded packaging.

Collector summary:
The Early Bird Certificate Package was a brilliant promotional solution born out of necessity and is one of the most important early pieces in the vintage Star Wars toy line.
