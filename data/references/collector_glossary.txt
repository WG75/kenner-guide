==============================
CORE COLLECTOR TERMS
==============================

MOC = Mint on Card  
Figure is still sealed on its original card.

OG = Original  
Can mean:
- Not a reproduction or bootleg  
- From the original vintage run  
- The first version of a figure before later variations  
Example: Luke Skywalker (original) vs later variants like Bespin, Hoth, Jedi, etc.

Mint / Minty = Like new condition  
Often overused in listings. “Minty” is informal slang.

Beater = Heavily worn loose figure  
Typically has paint loss, discolouration, loose limbs, or damage. Usually low value unless rare.

Loose = Not carded  
May or may not include accessories. Can also refer to loose limbs.

Rubs = Paint rubs, scuffs or marks on a figure.

Melt Marks = Production flaw or damage marks  
Common on Spanish Poch / PBP figures, but can appear on others. Can also be caused by play wear.

Pews = Slang for blasters / guns  
Used online to avoid moderation filters on words like gun or rifle.

==============================
GROUP / SET TERMS
==============================

Cantina Crew = Informal grouping of cantina characters  
Core figures:
- Greedo  
- Walrusman  
- Snaggletooth  
- Hammerhead  

Extended usage may include:
- Blue Snaggletooth  
- Jawa  
- Luke  
- Ben  
- Han  
- Chewbacca  
- Stormtrooper  

Hoth Figures = Figures associated with Hoth (Empire Strikes Back)  
Examples include:
- Imperial Stormtrooper (Hoth Battle Gear)  
- Rebel Soldier (Hoth Battle Gear)  
- Han Solo (Hoth Outfit)  
- Leia (Hoth Outfit)  
- Rebel Commander  
- AT-AT Driver  
- 2-1B  
- R2-D2 (Sensorscope)  
- C-3PO (Removable Limbs)  
- Luke Skywalker (Hoth Battle Gear)  
- AT-AT Commander  

==============================
PROMOTION / PACKAGING TERMS
==============================

Mail Aways = Promotional items obtained by sending off proof of purchase

POP (Proof of Purchase) = Logos printed on cardbacks/boxes  
Used for promotions where collectors cut them out and mailed them to Kenner or Palitoy.  
Items were typically returned in plain packaging (box with sealed bag), not retail packaging.

==============================
RESTORATION / CONDITION TERMS
==============================

Touch Ups = Repainting or restoration  
Can be:
- Childhood repair  
- Collector restoration  
- Dealer enhancement  

May affect value depending on quality and disclosure.

Paint Errors = Factory paint mistakes  
Usually caused by misaligned masks or missing paint layers. Fairly common.

==============================
GRADING TERMS
==============================

Graded = Figure professionally assessed and sealed in a protective case  
- Usually encased in acrylic (“slabbed”)  
- Given a condition score  
- Can increase buyer confidence and resale value  

Common grading companies:
- AFA (Action Figure Authority) – strongest resale recognition  
- CAS (Collector Archive Services) – high quality display cases  
- UKG (UK Graders) – main UK option  
- TGS (Toy Graders) – UK-based alternative  

Collector notes:
- AFA is generally seen as the resale benchmark  
- UKG preferred in the UK to avoid shipping risk  
- CAS often praised for presentation  

Slabbed = Graded and sealed in a protective case  
Some collectors dislike slabbed figures as they cannot be handled.

Non-Graded / Raw = Not professionally graded  
- Can be loose or carded  
- Preferred by collectors who want to handle and inspect figures  
- Usually cheaper than graded examples  

==============================
COO COLLECTING (ADVANCED)
==============================

COO = Country of Origin stamp on a figure

Deleted COO Types:

Smooth Deleted COO  
COO fully removed and surface appears flat and smooth. Letters were filled and polished in the mould.

Melted Down COO  
COO partially visible and appears melted. Usually caused by scraping and smoothing, not actual melting.

Scarred Out COO  
Deep rough scar where COO was. Caused by filling the mould area (often with a tin/lead mixture).  
Often shows bubbles or rough texture.

Blank Raised Bar COO  
COO drilled out, leaving a flat blank area. Sometimes used to apply a new COO.

COO Behaviour Notes:
- Fill material can degrade or break over time  
- Variations can occur due to mould wear or repair  
- Example: half-scarred Snaggletooth likely caused by mould damage  

Unique Example:
Macao Biker Scout  
COO stamped over scar, later removed again (“scarred out the scar”)

==============================
COO FAMILIES
==============================

COO Family = Group of figures made from the same mould  
- Even if COO markings are altered  
- Even if stamps differ  

Key principles:
- Same mould = same family  
- Visual differences often caused by plastic type or cooling  

Mould behaviour:
- Often produced in pairs  
- New moulds introduce slight variations  
- COO changes usually applied across paired moulds  

==============================
COO ERRORS
==============================

Common types:
- Short shots  
- Poor shots  
- Cooling errors  

Causes:
- Not enough plastic injected  
- Remnants from previous mould shots  
- Figure removed too early during cooling  

How to identify:

Common Sense Rule  
Removing only part of a COO usually makes no logical sense.  
True no-COO figures:
- Either fully removed  
- Or no markings at all  

Quantity Rule  
- One-off examples = likely production error  
- Multiple examples = more likely legitimate variation  

==============================
FAKED COOS
==============================

Fake COO marks are becoming more common as demand increases.

Scammers may alter COO stamps to mimic rare variants.

Collectors should be cautious, especially with rare or unusual COO examples.
