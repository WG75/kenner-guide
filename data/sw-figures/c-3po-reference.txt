Figure Name: C-3PO

Character Overview:
C-3PO (See-Threepio) is a humanoid protocol droid in the Star Wars franchise. Designed for etiquette and translation, he is fluent in over six million forms of communication. He appears across the entire Skywalker Saga and is portrayed by Anthony Daniels.

Collector Names / Aliases:
C-3PO, Threepio, See-Threepio, Golden Droid

Release Information:
- Released: 1978–1985
- Appears on: Star Wars, Empire Strikes Back, Return of the Jedi cardbacks
- Early Bird mail-away figure (1978)
- Part of original Wave A 12 Back figures

GENERAL PRODUCTION NOTES

- C-3PO is chrome plated over base plastic
- Finish varies depending on factory and process
- Chrome degradation is common:
  - wear
  - dulling
  - flaking

Important:
- Chrome condition is one of the biggest value factors
- Identifying variants requires combining:
  - COO
  - sculpt details
  - plating tone

--------------------------------------------------

CHROME VARIANTS (GENERAL)

Main Visual Types:
- Gold (standard Kenner look)
- Pale gold
- Silver
- Dual-tone (gold + silver mix)
- Dull bronze tone (aging or production variation)

Notes:
- Differences can come from:
  - plating process
  - factory
  - age / wear
- Some silver variants are factory-produced (not wear)

--------------------------------------------------

KADER

Notes:
- Early and continued production
- Standard Kenner gold appearance

Traits:
- Classic gold chrome
- Typical Kenner sculpt

--------------------------------------------------

UNITOY

Notes:
- Major production factory
- Wide COO variation across releases

Traits:
- Chrome tone varies from gold to duller shades
- Often grouped with Lili Ledy in early production crossover

--------------------------------------------------

LILI LEDY (MEXICO)

Key Variants:
- Gold
- Pale gold
- Silver
- Dual-tone (gold + silver)

Important Collector Notes:
- Chroming process in Mexico was inconsistent
- “Faded” look is often factory-applied, not wear
- Some figures show:
  - silver limbs + gold torso
  - mixed plating across body

COO Notes:
- May appear:
  - with COO
  - no COO
  - altered COO

Head Variants:
- Differences exist in facial sculpt and chrome tone

Collector Insight:
- Lili Ledy C-3PO is one of the most visually varied figures in the entire line

--------------------------------------------------

TAKARA (JAPAN)

Notes:
- Completely unique production method
- One of only a few figures made this way

Traits:
- Different internal construction (screw assembly)
- Distinct chrome tone
- High-quality finish compared to Kenner

--------------------------------------------------

COO DETAILS

Important Warning:
DO NOT rely only on COO to identify C-3PO variants.

Correct identification requires:
- chrome tone
- mould details
- plating behaviour
- assembly traits

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON IDENTIFICATION TRAITS

Look for:

- Chrome colour:
  - gold vs pale vs silver
- Consistency:
  - full body vs mixed tones
- COO marking:
  - present / removed / altered
- Sculpt differences:
  - especially face and torso details

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Assuming silver = worn gold (often incorrect)
- Ignoring Lili Ledy production differences
- Over-relying on COO alone
- Confusing factory variation with damage
