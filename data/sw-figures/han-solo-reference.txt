Figure Name: Han Solo

Character Overview:
Han Solo is a central character in the Star Wars franchise. First appearing in Star Wars (1977), he is a smuggler who pilots the Millennium Falcon with Chewbacca. Initially hired to transport Luke Skywalker and Obi-Wan Kenobi, he later joins the Rebel Alliance and plays a key role in the fight against the Empire. Portrayed by Harrison Ford.

Collector Names / Aliases:
Han, Han Solo, OG Han, Original Han, Small Head Han, Big Head Han

Release Information:
- Released: 1978–1985
- Appears on: Star Wars, Empire Strikes Back, Return of the Jedi cardbacks
- Early Bird mail-away figure (1978)
- Part of original Wave A 12 Back figures

--------------------------------------------------

KADER (COO Family 2) – EARLY PRODUCTION

Key Variant:
Small Head Han (Earliest Known Variant)

Variants:
- White moulded legs (fully oversprayed black)
- Transitional version (white legs + later face mask)
- Black moulded legs (standard later production)

Important Collector Insight:

The earliest Kader Han figures were produced with:
- White moulded arms AND legs
- Legs fully sprayed black after moulding

This appears inefficient at first, but likely follows a broader production rule:

Early ANH figures often had:
- Arms and legs moulded in the same base plastic colour

Examples:
- Luke → white limbs
- Greedo → green limbs

Theory:
Early figures may have been produced on shared sprue trees (arms + legs same plastic), similar to later bootleg production methods.

Production Change:
- Quickly shifted to black moulded legs + white arms (more efficient)

Identification Tips:
- Check peg holes for white plastic
- Look for paint rubs revealing white underneath
- Compare face paint masks for early vs later versions

--------------------------------------------------

GLASSLITE (BRAZIL)

Differences vs Kenner:

- Harder plastic
- Pale face, neck and hands
- Lighter reddish hair
- Lighter brown eyebrows
- Brown eyes (Kenner = black)
- Hand-painted details on hands
- Excess mould flashing

Paint & Finish:
- More visible QA variation
- Hand-painted + spray masked leg stripes
- Rougher finish compared to Kenner

Accessory Pairing:
- Solid black Jawa blaster (NOT Rebel blaster)

Important:
- Jawa blaster mould is identical to Leia silver blaster mould

Water Test:
- Both Kenner and Glasslite blasters float
- Neither sinks when tapped

--------------------------------------------------

PBP (SPAIN)

Notes:
- Uses modified Kenner/Unitoy moulds
- Often features COO alterations (scarred or removed)
- Colour variation common

Accessory Pairing:
- Typically Rebel blaster variants (M4 / M5 style)

--------------------------------------------------

GENERAL COLLECTOR NOTES

- Han Solo is heavily affected by mould + paint transitions
- Early production quirks are key to identification
- Leg mould colour is one of the most important tells
- Face paint masks help determine production stage

Common Mistakes:
- Assuming all Hans have black moulded legs
- Ignoring peg hole colour
- Confusing Glasslite with poor-condition Kenner
- Mixing Rebel blaster with Jawa blaster
