Figure Name: R2-D2 (Artoo-Detoo)

Character Overview:
R2-D2 (Artoo-Detoo) is an astromech droid in the Star Wars franchise. He appears across nearly all theatrical Star Wars films and is a companion to C-3PO, Luke Skywalker, Princess Leia and Obi-Wan Kenobi. The character is portrayed through a mix of practical effects, radio-controlled units and sound design.

Collector Names / Aliases:
R2, Artoo, Solid Dome, OG, O.G., Original, Early Bird R2, 12 Back R2

Release Information:
- Released: 1978–1981 (Solid Dome version)
- Transitioned to Sensorscope version in 1981
- Appears on: Star Wars and early Empire Strikes Back cardbacks
- Early Bird mail-away figure (1978)
- Part of original Wave A 12 Back figures

ACCESSORIES

Primary Accessories:
- None

Collector Note:
- Identification relies entirely on:
  - dome colour
  - sticker type
  - COO family
  - paint application
  - internal parts / screws

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR WARNINGS

R2-D2 is one of the most tampered-with figures in the entire line.

Reasons:
- Fully disassemblable without damage
- Parts easily swapped between figures
- Decades of “improved” or mixed examples

Common Problems:
- Mixed parts (“Frankenstein” figures)
- Reproduction stickers
- Misidentified variants
- False myths passed as fact

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MYTHS (IMPORTANT)

- “Golden dome” variant → FALSE  
  → This is lacquer degradation, not a factory variant

- “Early Bird specific figure” → FALSE  
  → Early Bird sets contain mixed factory figures

- “Dimple dome variant” → FALSE  
  → This is a moulding defect (short shot), not a variant

- “No COO solid dome R2 exists” → FALSE  
  → These are body swaps from Sensorscope figures

--------------------------------------------------

GENERAL PRODUCTION TRAITS

- Dome is vacuum metallised (silver finish)
- Highly prone to:
  - dulling
  - rubs
  - loss of shine

Collector Note:
- High dome “luster” = premium value

--------------------------------------------------

UNITOY (M1 / M5)

Key Traits:
- Widest variation due to low QC

Progression:
- Early: very dark blue dome (Early Bird / 12 backs)
- Later: mid blue dome (ESB era)
- Transition: small wedge Sensorscope sticker appears

Variants:
- 2-part halftone sticker (earliest)
- 1-part revised sticker
- Mid-blue dome + mid-blue legs
- Small wedge sticker transition figures

Notes:
- Some late figures appear rushed or inconsistent
- M5 family appears briefly with unclear production reasoning

--------------------------------------------------

POCH (SPAIN)

Key Traits:
- Mix of Asian parts (Unitoy / Kader)
- Variable quality and combinations

Visual Traits:
- Bright blue but dull chrome dome
- Crimson or purple-toned eye (paint pooling)
- Glossy mid-blue legs (often messy application)
- Misty / splattery paint

Important:
- Highly inconsistent
- Requires multiple traits to confirm

--------------------------------------------------

SMILE (M2)

Key Traits:
- Consistent production line

Progression:
- Dark blue dome → electric mid-blue dome

Features:
- Large crimson eye
- Consistent leg colour with minor variation
- Sticker grey darkens over time

--------------------------------------------------

KADER (M3 / M4 / M7)

Key Traits:
- One of the largest producers

Progression:
- Early: dark blue dome
- Later: mid-blue dome

Features:
- Matt mid-blue legs
- Large crimson eye
- Consistent sculpt and sticker layout

Special Note:
- 1977 torso mould later reused for R5-D4

--------------------------------------------------

LILI LEDY (MEXICO)

Key Traits:
- Unique sticker artwork
- Light-blue legs consistent across variants

Variants:
- Dark blue dome
- Dull blue dome (purple hue)
- Light blue dome (greenish tone)
- Mid-blue dome (possible PBP-linked version)

Sticker Types:
- Type A (blank rectangles)
- Type B (green-grey panels)
- Type C (light-grey panels)

Important:
- Porous sticker material → poor durability
- Some versions imported into Spain (PBP)

--------------------------------------------------

TAIWAN (M6)

Key Traits:
- 12 back only

Features:
- Dark blue → mid → green-blue dome variations
- Large orange-red eye
- Light-blue legs
- Halftone wedge sticker

--------------------------------------------------

DROID FACTORY PLAYSET

Key Traits:
- Completely different sculpt
- Fully disassemblable design
- Removable third leg

Sticker:
- Reverse artwork layout
- Straight vs wavy line variants

--------------------------------------------------

TAKARA WIND-UP R2 (JAPAN)

Key Traits:
- Wind-up walking mechanism
- Different sculpt and proportions

Variants:
- Japan COO (no Takara stamp)
- Japan COO (with Takara stamp)
- No COO version (second mould family)

Features:
- Larger dome differences
- Chrome button variation
- Transparent stickers (front & back)

--------------------------------------------------

STICKER GUIDE (CRITICAL)

Standard Kenner:
- 3-colour print (blue, black, grey)

Key Variants:
- Unitoy early → 2-part halftone sticker
- Taiwan → halftone wedge pattern
- Smile → darker wedge progression
- Kader → minimal design change, darker later grey
- Lili Ledy → completely unique artwork
- Glasslite → unique adaptation
- Droid Factory → reversed design

Important:
- Reproduction stickers are extremely common
- Some are designed to mimic ageing

--------------------------------------------------

SCREW & PART IDENTIFICATION

Key Principle:
Every part is factory-specific

Look at:
- screws
- internal parts
- mould marks (EPM / gate scars)

Important:
- Parts are frequently swapped
- Many figures on the market are mixed

Collector Rule:
If parts do not match known combinations → assume tampering

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Believing in “gold dome” variants
- Searching for a specific Early Bird version
- Ignoring part mismatching
- Trusting stickers without verification
- Not checking screws and internal parts
