Figure Name: Death Squad Commander

Alternate Name:
Star Destroyer Commander (later cardbacks)

Character Overview:
The Death Squad Commander is an Imperial officer figure released in the original 1978 Kenner line. The name was later changed to “Star Destroyer Commander” on subsequent releases, likely due to negative connotations around the original name. The figure represents a standard Imperial Navy officer seen aboard Star Destroyers.

Collector Names / Aliases:
Death Squad Commander, Star Destroyer Commander, DSC

Release Information:
- Released: 1978–1985
- Early name: Death Squad Commander
- Later name: Star Destroyer Commander
- Same sculpt used throughout name change

COO (COUNTRY OF ORIGIN) FAMILIES

Family I:
UNITOY (Hong Kong)

Traits:
- Early production
- Cleaner mould definition
- Standard paint application

--------------------------------------------------

Family II:
KADER / KADER China / Lili Ledy crossover

Traits:
- Slight variation in mould finish
- Minor differences in paint tone
- Transitional COO markings

--------------------------------------------------

Family III:
PBP (Spain)

Traits:
- Slightly different plastic tone
- Subtle sculpt and paint differences
- European distribution

--------------------------------------------------

IMPORTANT:
👉 COO alone is NOT enough to confirm a variant  
👉 Must also check paint and assembly traits

--------------------------------------------------

POCH VARIANT (IMPORTANT)

The Poch (Spanish) Death Squad Commander is one of the most notable variants.

Key Identifiers:

Chest Insignia:
- Red and blue bars reversed compared to Kenner
- Red often applied BEFORE blue (opposite of Kenner)

Paint Application:
- Rougher or “hand-finished” look
- Less precise edges

Boots and Gloves:
- “Dripped” paint appearance
- Less clean than Kenner finish

Additional Traits:
- Melt marks on torso back
- Paint inconsistencies across figure

Collector Note:
- Poch figures often show multiple small tells rather than one clear identifier

--------------------------------------------------

FACTORY / PRODUCTION OBSERVATIONS

- Produced across multiple factories (Unitoy, Kader, PBP, etc.)
- Minor sculpt consistency, but paint and finish vary
- Variants are subtle compared to other figures

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

To correctly identify a Death Squad Commander:

Check:

1. COO marking (leg stamp)
2. Chest insignia (order of paint layers)
3. Paint finish (clean vs rough)
4. Boot and glove paint quality
5. Plastic tone and mould clarity

Do NOT rely on:
- COO alone
- Blaster alone

--------------------------------------------------

NAMING VARIATION (IMPORTANT)

Early:
- Death Squad Commander

Later:

Collector Impact:

COMMON MISTAKES

- Assuming Star Destroyer Commander is a different figure
- Ignoring Poch paint differences
- Overvaluing blaster variation instead of figure traits
- Relying only on COO stamp
