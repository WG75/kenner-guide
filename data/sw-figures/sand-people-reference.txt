Figure Name: Tusken Raider (Sand People)

Character Overview:
Tusken Raiders, originally named “Sand People” on early cardbacks, are a nomadic and hostile species native to Tatooine. They wear layered robes, masks, and breathing apparatus, and are known for their use of the Gaderffii stick (staff weapon). They first appeared in Star Wars (1977).

Collector Names / Aliases:
Tusken Raider, Sand People, Sandperson, Tusken, TR, Raiders, Sandman, Sandmen

Release Information:
- Released: 1978–1985
- Appears on: Star Wars, Empire Strikes Back, Return of the Jedi cardbacks
- Originally carded as “Sand People”
- Name later standardised to Tusken Raider

--------------------------------------------------

ACCESSORIES

Primary Accessories:
- Vinyl Cape (main body cloak)
- Gaderffii Stick (staff weapon)

Accessory Matching Rules:
- Every Tusken should have BOTH cape and gaffi stick
- Cape + weapon pairing is critical for correct identification

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTES

Tusken Raider is a combination-based figure:

- Figure sculpt differences
- Vinyl cape variation
- Gaderffii stick mould variation

All three must align for proper identification.

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE (SOFT GOODS)

Material:
- Vinyl (not cloth like Jawa later versions)
- Beige / off-white colour

Construction:
- Cut vinyl sheet
- Multiple hole configurations (typically 2 or 3 holes)
- Worn over torso like a poncho

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE VARIANTS

Mould / Cut Variations (M1–M6+ observed):

Differences include:
- Hole count (2-hole, 3-hole variants)
- Hole spacing
- Shape of cut edges
- Thickness and flexibility
- Surface texture (smooth vs slightly grainy)

Material Behaviour:
- Can stiffen with age
- Discolouration common
- Warping possible

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY

Check:
1. Hole configuration
2. Shape and symmetry
3. Thickness
4. Age wear (yellowing, stiffness)

Do NOT rely on:
- Colour alone
- Hole count alone

--------------------------------------------------

GADERFFII STICK (GAFFI STICK)

General:
- Long staff weapon unique to Tusken Raider
- One of the most recognisable accessories in the line

--------------------------------------------------

MOULD VARIANTS

M1:
- Thinner shaft
- Defined “axe head”
- Cleaner mould finish

M2:
- Slightly thicker shaft
- Softer detail
- Variation in handle shape

M3:
- More simplified sculpt
- Less defined edges

Key Differences:
- Shaft thickness
- Head shape
- Mould seam lines
- Gate scar placement

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR VARIANTS

Observed colours:
- Dark brown
- Medium brown
- Reddish brown
- Grey-brown (faded or factory variation)

Notes:
- Colour variation often due to plastic mix and ageing
- Some variation between factories

--------------------------------------------------

FACTORY VARIATION (FIGURE)

COO Families include:
- Hong Kong (various factories)
- Taiwan
- No COO variants
- Possible Poch / Lili Ledy variants

Key Figure Traits:
- Mask sculpt differences
- Eye paint variation
- Belt colour variation
- Robe paint tone differences

--------------------------------------------------

PRODUCTION LOGIC

Tusken Raider production shows:

- Early mould variation across multiple factories
- Independent evolution of cape cuts and weapon moulds
- No strict 1:1 pairing rules like some figures

Instead:
👉 Variants must be judged as combinations

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Mixing incorrect cape with figure
- Assuming all capes are identical
- Ignoring gaffi stick mould differences
- Over-relying on COO markings
- Using reproduction sticks (very common)

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

Tusken Raider is:

- Less about single “rare variant”
- More about correct matching combinations

Unlike Jawa:
- No single accessory dominates value
- Balance of all components matters

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY (CRITICAL)

To correctly identify a Tusken Raider:

You must evaluate:

1. Figure sculpt (mask + body)
2. Cape variant (cut + holes)
3. Gaderffii stick mould + colour
4. COO marking

All three components must align.

--------------------------------------------------

NAMING NOTE

- Early cardbacks: “Sand People”
- Later standard: “Tusken Raider”

Both refer to the same figure.
