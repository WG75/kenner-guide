Figure Name: Ben (Obi-Wan) Kenobi

Character Overview:
Obi-Wan Kenobi, also known as Ben Kenobi, is a Jedi Master in the Star Wars franchise. He appears in the original trilogy as a mentor to Luke Skywalker and is portrayed by Alec Guinness. In the vintage Kenner line, he is represented as an older Jedi wearing brown robes and carrying a lightsaber.

Collector Names / Aliases:
Ben, Obi-Wan, Old Ben, Farm Ben, 12 Back Ben

Release Information:
- Released: 1978 onwards
- Part of original 12 Back figures
- Appears across Star Wars and Empire Strikes Back cardbacks

--------------------------------------------------

ACCESSORIES

Primary Accessories:
- Vinyl Cape / Cloth Cape (brown)
- Lightsaber (blue)
  - Double Telescoping (DT)
  - Single Telescoping

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL COLLECTOR NOTES

Ben Kenobi is one of the most complex early figures due to:

- Cape variations (material, cut, holes)
- Early DT lightsaber pairing
- Colour variation in sabers
- Reproduction cape issues

Cape authenticity is one of the biggest pitfalls in collecting this figure.

--------------------------------------------------

FIGURE VARIANT FAMILIES (COO BASED)

Family I:
- Unitoy / Poch

Family II:
- Kader

Family III:
- Lili Ledy

Family IV:
- Taiwan

Family V:
- Smile

(Exact family traits depend on COO markings and sculpt details)

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE (EXTREMELY IMPORTANT)

Material:
- Thin vinyl (early)
- Cloth (later variants)

Key Identification Points:
- Number of holes (neck area)
- Hole spacing and alignment
- Shape and cut of cape
- Thickness and flexibility
- Edge finishing

Common Variations:
- Single hole
- Double hole
- Multi-hole (factory variation)
- Rough / misaligned cuts (legitimate)

Important:
- Capes vary by factory
- No single “correct” cape without matching figure traits

--------------------------------------------------

CAPE WARNINGS

- Reproduction capes are extremely common
- Some are artificially aged
- Hole placement is often incorrect on repros
- Material is often too thick or too clean

Collector Rule:
Cape must match:
- factory
- era
- figure COO
- wear pattern

--------------------------------------------------

LIGHTSABER (KEY IDENTIFICATION AREA)

Ben Kenobi uses BLUE sabers only.

--------------------------------------------------

DOUBLE TELESCOPING (DT) SABER

Used in earliest releases (12 backs)

Colour:
- Blue (very rare compared to Luke yellow and Vader red)

Moulds:
- M1 (circled DT)
- M2 (lettered DT)

Key Traits:
- Inner telescoping blade
- Fragile, often broken
- Pale/translucent colours

Notes:
- M1 likely earlier than M2
- Found across early Kader, Unitoy, Smile runs

--------------------------------------------------

SINGLE TELESCOPING SABERS

Used after DT production stopped

Major Moulds:

M1:
- Flat transition
- Lettered hilt
- Early production (all factories)

M2:
- Flat transition
- Unlettered
- Unitoy dominant

M3:
- Thin tip
- Later Unitoy production

M5:
- Round transition
- Kader / Smile

M7:
- Taiwan-specific mould

--------------------------------------------------

COLOUR VARIATION (BLUE SABERS)

Observed:
- Light blue
- Mid blue
- Dark blue
- Slight translucent variants

Important:
- Colour varies by factory
- Fading and ageing affects appearance

--------------------------------------------------

PRODUCTION LOGIC (IMPORTANT)

DT sabers were:
- Produced in shared moulds
- Intended for multiple factories
- Pulled early from production

After this:
- Factories produced their own moulds
- Variation increased significantly

--------------------------------------------------

IDENTIFICATION STRATEGY (CRITICAL)

To correctly identify a Ben Kenobi:

You must combine:

1. COO marking
2. Cape type (material + holes)
3. Saber type (DT or single + mould)
4. Paint details (face, hair)
5. Plastic tone and finish

COO alone is NOT enough.

--------------------------------------------------

COMMON MISTAKES

- Assuming all Ben figures had the same cape
- Pairing wrong saber with figure
- Trusting cape without checking holes/material
- Confusing repro capes with originals
- Assuming DT sabers are common (they are rare)

--------------------------------------------------

COLLECTOR INSIGHT

Ben Kenobi is one of the best examples of:

- Early production inconsistency
- Factory overlap
- Accessory-driven value differences

DT Ben figures are among the most desirable early releases.
